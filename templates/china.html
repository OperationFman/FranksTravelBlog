People's Republic of China

2017, October

Federation University gave me the life changing opportunity to travel to south China in Shaoguan to study a single intense course (Web development actually) with a Chinese University.

I made life long friends, saw the world in a way I didnt know was possible.

Watch my personal Music Video in China

Study Abroad in Shaoguan


My classmates and I flew into Guangzhou and took the train north to Shaoguan. The University we came to was absolutely massive and more like a small city than a school.
We got to meet the locals at an introductory dinner and settled into our new accommodation with no time to waste as tomorrow classes began.

This was an accelerated course so instead of one lecture and one tutorial per week we had both in one day, everyday, for the next 10 days plus assignments.

I actually loved this style of learning and it meant every minute counted.

I forged great friendships during this intense time, not only with the local chinese but my Australian mates. To this day (Writing in 2020) I still talk to just about all of them.

Extra Curricular Activities


Each weekend between classes was something exciting.
The first weekend we had mixed martial arts training, Lion Dancing and english spelling B’s which I absolutely nailed because I've actually spoken english most of my life.

Other times we ventured into Shaoguan city itself, a small village of 7 million people.

Journey to Hunan


After finishing the course our class said goodbye to our sister University and headed north-west toward Hunan.

Along the way we stayed in breathtaking river towns with rickety wooden single file bridges to cross the icy water.

We visited a village completely run by women. Men in this culture have to spend years proving their worth.

We visited the University of Changda, where Chairman Mao began the great march.

Tianmen Mountain and the Worlds Largest Glass Suspension Bridge


Next we visited the world's largest glass suspension bridge which I still can’t remember crossing.

Finally we adventured Tianman mountain, known for extreme sports and being the inspiration for the 2009 movie Avatar.
