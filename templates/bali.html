Indonesia, (Bali)

2019, February

Keegan needed to get away from it all and what better place for broke aussies?

We planned this trip in 4 days and boy did we get burnt,
(By the sun I mean, my legs still feel like leather).

Watch my personal Music Video in Bali

White Water Rafting


We arrived at ChristChurch on the southern island, tired and sore. But soon we’d be in our hire car and headed for Tekapo, unless of course something unforeseen stopped us.

Yep, I made the booking with rentalcars.com making sure the company we originally booked with allowed for under 25 year olds. Yet when they changed our booking to a different company they elected to omit that special request even when I asked them to make sure.
We did receive a partial refund since it was their fault (and I had proof) but it was a headache I hoped to avoid.

Luckily we got a new hire with a new company and journeys off to the west just fine (After trying the local cuisine, Maccas (Or as they call it in kiwi land, McDonalds).

Big Garden Corner


After a rather dangerous drive (We were pretty tired) we made it to the stunningly blue lake of Tekapo, known best for the old Shepherd's Cottage sitting by the banks with it’s vivid purple flowers spoked everywhere.
It’s said it’s impossible to take a bad photo of this monument and I believe it.

We spent the night skipping rocks on the lake and giggling like schoolgirls at baby ducks.

Peace and Freedom


The next day was predominantly spent driving.
We first visited the top of a mountain nearby tekapo for a hot breakfast then drove through valleys and mountains on our way to the famous Queenstown.

After that was a brief and very easy walk up to Mount Cook. Luckily we brought plenty of water or we would have been severely dehydrated and sunburnt
(Spoiler, we didn't).

When we finally arrived we saw a stupidly long line to a single burger joint which we unanimously dismissed as a silly place to visit.

Uluwatu Temple


We woke up early and walked into town to meet up with other tourists and took a bus out to Arrowhead.
Quadbikes was the agenda and boy was it a blast. We rode through private land up and down rivers and raced across paddocks. The weather turned maybe 3 times so we ended up both wet, dusty and overheated all in one morning.

After getting back and a much needed shower we headed up the mountain to the famous Luge ride. It’s a neat little go kart track that uses the power of gravity and what looks unsafe speeds (But actually feels just fine).

That night after Dods snoring woke Sam, Wally and I up we snuck out to visit that burger joint.

Ferg Burger as we came to know it, is the tale of a man who ran his own little burger cart that became so well known to tourists (Who spread its name around the world) it became a must have.

If you’ve never been to NZ it is a must have. Food coma for days, loved it.
