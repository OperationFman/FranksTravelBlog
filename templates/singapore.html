Singapore

2019, August

After a spectacular few days in Malaysia it was time to go broke or go home in the famous pirate den of Singapore.

Onward, to the Economic Tiger of Asia.

Watch my personal Music Video in Singapore

The Merlion


Given I was still quite sick I elected to instead take a bus to Singapore rather than flying with the others (Pretty severe ear pain). I enjoyed the comfort but the driver had some kind of a burp problem and I was stuck right behind him, yuck!

The next morning we all headed to the Orchard shopping centre and while having lunch a local sat with us offering a lucky-dip for some keychain accessories.
After that I was forced into some shopping by the thots, followed by visiting every fresh orange juice dispenser on Orchard street while riding electric scooters.

We grabbed a grab (taxi) and got off at the famous Merlion, at least we thought, when we asked the driver to take us to the Merlion we assumed it was 5 minutes away and by the water, not the 20 minutes away and in the middle of a park, life is weird sometimes.

Heat is immense this hour of the day so we loaded up on ice cream and whinged about the cost, after Malaysia this place is a mad rip off.

The park was neat with water features and chairlifts, I wanted to bungee jump but Frank didn’t so we decided not to.

Gondola Tour of the City


After that we boarded the Singapore Gondola adenturing far across the island. Trundling past towers, over harbours, along Universal Studios and several parks.
All the while we jammed to Life is a highway on my tinny phone speakers and took selfies.

I don’t seem to have taken a photo but the amount of large ships out to sea was unfathomable, it reminded me of the planet Coruscant from Star Wars.

Later Keegan came out and we hit the scooters again for a little 1-on-1 adventure through the city late into the night.

Cloud Forest


On our last night we had to see the mighty Gardens by the Bay, bordered by the Marina Bay towers.
We went late (Resulting in us not being able to ENTER, but nonetheless stunning). The trees glow vividly with colours and spectators take awkward up-the-snoz photos, luckily they are hard to see in this lighting.

Surrounding the trees is a stretch of animal reserves and aquariums stalked by cats and other cheeky animals.

We had an enormous dinner in the sweltering heat and checked out the entire bay-walk area, revelling in the fact none of us could really afford to do anything ‘exciting’, but, knowing one day we will.

All in all Singapore (And Malaysia) were some of the greatest countries I've ever enjoyed, I’d love to do it again with some sort of a budget but I think the basic-ness of it is what made it special.
